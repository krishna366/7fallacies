The 7 Fallacies of Generative AI / LLM-Based Automation

1. “One-Shot Generation” Fallacy
Assumes that a single LLM call can produce a complete, correct, and contextually accurate output without the need for iterative refinement or multi-turn reasoning.
2. “Speed Over Substance” Fallacy
Prioritizes generation speed at the cost of depth, contextual accuracy, or validation, leading to outputs that may be fast but flawed.
3. “Zero Preprocessing” Fallacy
Neglects the importance of prework—such as summarization, embedding, or indexing—assuming the model can operate optimally without structured context or grounding.
4. “Minimal Input Sufficiency” Fallacy
Assumes that terse inputs (like function or action names) are enough for the LLM to generate meaningful outputs, ignoring the need for rich semantics, descriptions, or relational context.
5. “No Reasoning Needed” Fallacy
Disregards the importance of intermediate reasoning steps, planning, or tool-use scaffolding, expecting direct answers from LLMs without verifying the logic behind them.
6. “Prompt Has All the Answers” Fallacy
Overestimates the completeness and clarity of user prompts, assuming they contain everything needed for accurate generation, without needing clarification, context expansion, or additional data fetching.
7. “Post-Generation Checks Are Optional” Fallacy
Ignores the necessity of validating the output for correctness, alignment with intent, or consistency, assuming LLMs are inherently reliable without guardrails.
